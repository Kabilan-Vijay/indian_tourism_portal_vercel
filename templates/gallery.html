{% if place.place_name == 'Taj Mahal' %}

The Taj Mahal, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the south bank of the Yamuna river in the Indian city of Agra. It was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan to house the tomb of his favorite wife, Mumtaz Mahal. The Taj Mahal is widely recognized as "the jewel of Muslim art in India" and one of the universally admired masterpieces of the world's heritage.

🏗️ Built: 1632-1653
👑 Commissioned by: Shah Jahan
🏛️ UNESCO World Heritage Site
{% elif place.place_name == 'Varanasi Ghats' %}

Varanasi, also known as Benares or Kashi, is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world and a major religious hub in India. The ghats along the Ganges River are sacred bathing places where Hindus come to perform rituals, pray, and cremate their loved ones. The city is considered the spiritual capital of India and attracts millions of pilgrims annually.

🌊 Sacred River: Ganges
🙏 Hindu Pilgrimage Site
🏛️ Ancient City: 3,000+ years old
{% elif place.place_name == 'Meenakshi Temple' %}

The Meenakshi Temple, also known as Meenakshi Amman Temple, is a historic Hindu temple located in the city of Madurai in Tamil Nadu. Dedicated to Goddess Meenakshi (an avatar of Goddess Parvati) and her consort Lord Shiva, this temple is renowned for its stunning Dravidian architecture, intricate sculptures, and vibrant festivals. It's one of the most important temples in South India.

🕉️ Dedicated to: Goddess Meenakshi
🏛️ Dravidian Architecture
🎭 Famous Festivals: Meenakshi Kalyanam
{% elif place.place_name == 'Ooty Hill Station' %}

Ooty, also known as Udhagamandalam, is a popular hill station in the Nilgiri Hills of Tamil Nadu. Famous for its tea plantations, botanical gardens, and colonial architecture, Ooty offers breathtaking views of valleys and mountains. The town was established by the British as a summer retreat and remains a favorite destination for nature lovers and honeymooners.

🏔️ Elevation: 2,240 meters
☕ Famous for: Tea Plantations
🌸 Botanical Garden: Ooty Botanical Gardens
{% elif place.place_name == 'Hampi Ruins' %}

Hampi, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is the ruins of the ancient city of Vijayanagara, the former capital of the Vijayanagara Empire. Located in Karnataka, Hampi is renowned for its stunning landscape dotted with numerous monuments, temples, and ruins that showcase the grandeur of medieval South Indian architecture and culture.

🏛️ UNESCO World Heritage Site
👑 Ancient Capital: Vijayanagara Empire
🗿 Stone Architecture
{% elif place.place_name == 'Rajasthan Forts' %}

Rajasthan is home to some of the most magnificent forts in India, showcasing the rich history and architectural brilliance of Rajputana. From the majestic Amber Fort to the imposing Mehrangarh Fort, these structures tell tales of valor, romance, and royal splendor. Each fort has its unique story and architectural style.

🏰 Rajputana Architecture
⚔️ Symbol of Rajput Valor
👑 Royal Heritage
{% elif place.place_name == 'Rann of Kutch' %}

The Rann of Kutch is a vast salt desert located in the Kutch district of Gujarat. During the monsoon season, it transforms into a mesmerizing white landscape, and during winter, it becomes a venue for the famous Rann Utsav festival. This unique geographical feature offers an otherworldly experience with its stark beauty and cultural significance.

🏜️ Salt Desert Landscape
🎪 Rann Utsav Festival
🌙 White Desert Experience
